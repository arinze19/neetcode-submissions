Graph traversals are O(V + E) 
V = visited nodes 
E = checked edges 
(V + E) because for each node we check its surrounding edges making the operation sequential (V + E) and not nested (V * E)
Directed Acyclic Graph (DAG): graph does not contain cycles
Directed Graph: arrows point in one direction 
Unidirected Graph: arrows point in multiple directions
Tree: A connected graph without cycles
Graphs are ideal for modeling and analyzing relationships between pairs of objects.

Related Algorithms
- Khan: Used to topological sort a graph
- Union Find: Detecting cycles between two nodes in a graph
- Bellman-Ford: 
    * Can't be used in a negative weight cycle
- Dijkstra: Like a BFS but with priority queue (heap)
- Minimum Spanning Trees: Kruskal or Prim's Algorithm
- Multi-source BFS: Multi source shortest
